About

Background

I spent 7 years in roles across SaaS companies: FinTech, supply chain, cybersecurity, entertainment. My role was always the same: identify where systems break, fix them, and make sure they stay fixed. I built processes, led teams, and designed workflows.

However, I noticed something...the symptoms kept returning because the knowledge that fixed them in the first place never got recorded.

How I got into documentation

At Traze, I was brought in to help with their documentation. But the real problem wasn't a process—it was that the only person who understood the software was the CEO. So I built a knowledge base from scratch. 250+ articles. I captured what lived in his head and made it structural.

Philosophy

On clarity: Good documentation doesn't explain everything. It explains what will matter later. It strips away the noise and keeps the signal. This requires judgment—knowing what people actually need versus what sounds comprehensive.

On process: Documentation isn't a project. It's a system that needs integration into how work happens. If it sits outside the workflow, it dies. So I design handoff templates, establish documentation standards, and build it into the rhythm of how teams operate.

On communication: The goal of writing is not to display knowledge. It's to make something possible. Can a new hire onboard faster? Can a support team answer questions without calling engineering? Can a client make decisions with better information? If the answer isn't yes, the documentation failed, no matter how well it's written.
